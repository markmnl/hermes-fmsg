Message hierarchy

      
Lead with the agent outcome and context model. Federation and
        self-hosting strengthen the story; protocol internals are proof, not the
        opening pitch.

      

        
01
Give your agent an address
A stable identity that humans and other agents can contact directly.

        
02
Threads become context
Roots map to sessions; branches preserve ancestry instead of losing the conversation.

        
03
Messages survive downtime
Live push plus catch-up means the gateway does not have to be online at send time.

        
04
Delegate identity safely
Agent-specific keys can expire, be IP-restricted, rotated, and revoked.

        
05
Choose hosted or self-hosted
Start quickly, or control the domain, storage, quotas, retention, and policy yourself.

      

    


    

      

        
Claims to use

        
    
          
  • “Operator-controlled limits.” Accurate for self-hosting.
    • 
          
  • “No per-message platform metering.” Distinguishes messaging cost from model cost.
    • 
          
  • “Transport encryption and encrypted hosted storage.” Describe the layer actually provided.
    • 
          
  • “Domain-authorised sender verification.” Explains the trust model without absolutes.
    • 
          
  • “Developer preview.” Sets the right expectation for version 0.1.0.
    • 
        

      

      

        
Claims to avoid

        
    
          
  • “No limits.” Hardware, quotas, bandwidth, and abuse controls still exist.
    • 
          
  • “Free forever without limits.” State the hosted allowance and policy instead.
    • 
          
  • “End-to-end encrypted.” Do not imply user-held E2EE keys when describing TLS and at-rest encryption.
    • 
          
  • “Impossible to spoof.” Explain verification properties rather than promise perfection.
    • 
          
  • “Official Hermes plugin.” It is an independent community plugin.
